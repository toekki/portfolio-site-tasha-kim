BEFORE YOU BUY

2020 has brought some significant changes to how we make purchases, and online buying has become the preferred way to shop for many out of convenience and safety.

Seeing wall art on a screen can look quite beautiful; however, the common question lies if it would work in the space where it may be displayed.

Artsee Decor assures art lovers by providing the AR [augmented reality] function. Now there is no more guessing!

THE CHALLENGE

Create a responsive e-commerce web app where the art is respectfully displayed for consumers to easily find products that match their needs and to assure that they are making the right choice.

BRAND GUIDELINES

VISION

Art is everywhere.

It is difficult for many creatives to get noticed for their work, while it is also difficult for those who appreciate art to find what they are looking for. On top of all that, even if the customer considers that lovely piece of art - Will they commit?

Artsee Decor unites artists and art lovers to one place, creating a pleasant and reassuring experience with the use of AR (augmented reality). Customers can easily preview fabulous works from independent artists in their very own space, allowing them to see how it harmonizes with their environment. They never have to step into a store and can conveniently shop for art that are from different cultures and backgrounds from anywhere, anytime, right from their smartphone or tablet!

How awesome is that!? Very awesome.

ARTHOUSE

PROS

  • AR function available

  • Free native app download

  • Large selection of wall art

CONS

  • Users must go through the entire AR process to view the artwork details, cost, size, etc.

  • Society6 is a website where about anyone can sell their art, and the products are not of great quality

iOS app

PROS

  • Free native app download

CONS

  • Users must go through the entire AR process to view the artwork details, cost, size, etc.

USABILITY TESTING

5 PARTICIPANTS

After analyzing all the user notes taken from each of the video reviews, there were no critical issues but helpful suggestions. I made some cosmetic changes from the feedback, but the more significant pain points are mentioned below.

(3) Participants

While three of the testers were excited over the AR function, they were not clear of the AR symbol and overlooked the AR button when present.

Iteration

  • Introduced the AR feature and button in a modal screen at the start of the website usage.

  • Made the AR button more visible where available.

  • Option to animate the icon.

(1) Participant

Suggested a confirmation screen after a purchase to complete the overall experience.

Iteration

  • Added the confirmation screen at the end.

(2) Participants

Mentioned that the navigation menu list was too long, and it clearly showed on the video the time it took them to read it all.

Iteration

  • Reduced the list but editing any of the menus that overlapped one another.

RESPONSIVE DESIGN

Artsee Decor is a responsive web app where the users can use the AR function on any mobile device to view wall art in their space. While the augmented reality function does not work on a desktop, users can easily display images on their desktop of pictures saved from their mobile devices.

WHAT WENT WELL

  • I learned from my participants during the usability testing that the AR symbol is not as familiar as I assumed. The feedback helped me create a modal to introduce the feature and the AR symbol.

  • Using familiar UI patterns made it easy for testers to navigate my prototype smoothly.

ROOM FOR IMPROVEMENT

  • More user testing would be beneficial.

  • Reduce time spent in creating user flow diagrams.

WHAT DIDN'T GO WELL

  • Not always being present while users test the prototype is sometimes challenging, as I am not there to remind them to follow the given tasks. Some participants were quite distracted.

  • It took more time than expected in creating the logomark; however, I am happy with the result.

NEXT STEPS

  • Continue strengthening UX skills.

  • Add other payment option screen designs.
